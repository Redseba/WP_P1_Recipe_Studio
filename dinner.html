Cheesy Chicken & Broccoli Casserole


Main Ingredients

1½ lbs chicken breast, boneless and skinless, cut into 1-inch pieces

16 oz frozen broccoli florets, thawed and drained

1 can (10.75 oz) cream of chicken soup

8 oz American cheese or cheddar, cut into cubes

½ cup water

1 box (6 oz) Stove Top stuffing mix for chicken

½ cup melted butter (for stuffing topping)


Instructions

Step 1: Preheat oven to 400°F.

Step 2: Prepare the stuffing mix according to package directions using the melted butter. Set aside.

Step 3: In a 9x13-inch baking dish, combine chicken pieces, broccoli, cream of chicken soup, ½ cup water, and cheese cubes. Stir to mix well.

Step 4: Spread the prepared stuffing evenly over the top of the chicken mixture.

Step 5: Bake for 30 minutes until chicken is cooked through and topping is golden brown.

Let rest for 5 minutes, serve, and enjoy!

Creamy Chicken Alfredo


Main Ingredients

3–4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (about 1.5 lbs)

4 cloves garlic, minced

1 cup low-sodium chicken broth

8 oz cream cheese, softened

1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese

1 cup heavy cream

12 oz fettuccine or linguine pasta

2 tbsp olive oil (for searing chicken)

Salt and pepper to taste

Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)


Linguine Pasta (optional, from scratch)

2 cups all-purpose flour

3 large eggs, room temperature

½ tsp salt

1 tbsp olive oil (optional, for softer dough)

1–2 tbsp water (if needed)


Pasta Instructions (skip if not needed)

Step 1: Pile flour on a clean surface and create a wide well in the center.

Step 2: Crack eggs into the well. Add salt and olive oil if using.

Step 3: Using a fork, gently beat the eggs and gradually incorporate flour from the sides.

Step 4: Mix until a shaggy dough forms. Add water if too dry, or more flour if too sticky.

Step 5: Knead on a lightly floured surface for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.

Step 6: Wrap in plastic wrap and rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.

Step 7: Cut dough into 4 equal pieces. Keep unused pieces covered.

Step 8: Flatten one piece and run through a pasta roller on the widest setting. Fold into thirds and repeat 2–3 times.

Step 9: Continue through progressively thinner settings until you reach setting 5 or 6 for linguine.

Step 10: Cut into linguine using a cutter attachment, or hand-slice into ¼-inch strips.

Step 11: Dust with flour, toss to separate, and let dry on a floured baking sheet for 15–30 minutes.

Step 12: Cook fresh linguine in salted boiling water for 2–4 minutes.


Instructions

Step 1: Slice chicken breasts in half horizontally to create thinner cutlets. Season both sides with salt and pepper.

Step 2: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chicken 4–5 minutes per side until golden and cooked through. Remove and set aside.

Step 3: In the same skillet, sauté minced garlic for 1 minute until fragrant.

Step 4: Add chicken broth and bring to a simmer, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom.

Step 5: Reduce heat to low and stir in cream cheese until melted and smooth.

Step 6: Whisk in heavy cream and Parmesan cheese. Simmer gently for 3–4 minutes until sauce thickens.

Step 7: Cook pasta according to package directions while sauce simmers. Drain well.

Step 8: Slice the cooked chicken into strips.

Step 9: Add drained pasta to the sauce and toss to coat.

Serve topped with sliced chicken and garnish with fresh parsley if desired. Enjoy!

Chiles Rellenos


Main Ingredients

1 tbsp vegetable oil

1 tsp chili powder

½ tsp onion powder

¼ tsp granulated garlic

1½ cups chicken broth

1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce

1 tsp adobo sauce (from canned chipotle peppers)

1 bay leaf

1 tbsp lime juice

Salt and pepper to taste

6 poblano peppers

8 oz Muenster or Oaxaca cheese, cut into 12 cubes

6 wooden toothpicks or small skewers

1½ cups all-purpose flour (divided)

1¼ cups cornstarch

2 tsp baking powder

1 tsp salt

1½ cups light beer (lager)

3 quarts vegetable or peanut oil (for frying)


Instructions

Step 1: Heat oil in a saucepan over medium-high heat. Add chili powder, onion powder, and granulated garlic. Cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.

Step 2: Add chicken broth, tomato sauce, adobo sauce, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 12–15 minutes until reduced to 2 cups.

Step 3: Stir in lime juice, season with salt and pepper, and remove from heat. Discard bay leaf and keep warm.

Step 4: Adjust oven rack 6 inches from broiler and preheat broiler. Line a baking sheet with foil.

Step 5: Place poblanos on the sheet and broil 3–5 minutes per side until skins blister.

Step 6: Let cool slightly, then carefully peel off loose skin. Leave stems intact.

Step 7: Make a 3-inch vertical slit down one side of each pepper. Carefully remove seeds and membranes.

Step 8: Place 2 cheese cubes inside each pepper. Close the opening and secure with toothpicks.

Step 9: Place ½ cup flour in a shallow dish for dredging.

Step 10: In a bowl, combine remaining 1 cup flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and salt. Whisk in beer until smooth.

Step 11: Heat oil in a deep pot to 375°F.

Step 12: Dredge each stuffed pepper in the ½ cup flour, shaking off excess.

Step 13: Dip each pepper in batter to coat evenly.

Step 14: Carefully place in hot oil and fry for 4–5 minutes, turning frequently, until golden brown and crispy.

Step 15: Drain on a wire rack, remove toothpicks, and sprinkle with salt.

Serve warm with the ranchera sauce and enjoy!
