Classic Italian Sandwich


Main Ingredients

4 hoagie rolls or Italian buns

2 tbsp butter

24 slices Genoa salami

12 slices mortadella

24 slices pepperoni

12 slices honey ham

8 oz provolone cheese, sliced

½ head iceberg lettuce, shredded

3 tomatoes, sliced

1 onion, thinly sliced

½ cup mayonnaise

½ cup mustard

1 cup hot olive muffuletta spread (optional)

½ cup Italian dressing


Instructions

Step 1: Preheat oven to 350°F. Split hoagie rolls and lightly toast in the oven for 5 minutes.

Step 2: Spread mayonnaise and mustard on both sides of each roll.

Step 3: Layer shredded lettuce, tomato slices, and onion on the bottom half of each roll.

Step 4: Fold and layer salami, mortadella, pepperoni, and ham evenly among the sandwiches.

Step 5: Top with provolone cheese slices.

Step 6: Add muffuletta spread if using.

Step 7: Drizzle Italian dressing over the meat and cheese.

Close the sandwiches, slice in half, serve, and enjoy!

Spicy Thai Chicken Bowl


Main Ingredients

3–4 Persian cucumbers, thinly sliced

3 tbsp rice vinegar

1 tsp sugar or honey

1 tsp sesame oil

Toasted sesame seeds

¼ cup soy sauce (low sodium)

1–2 tsp sriracha or chili paste

2 tsp sweet chili sauce

1 tsp honey

1 lb ground chicken

¼ cup hoisin sauce

2 tsp fresh ginger, minced

2 cloves garlic, finely chopped

1 bag Asian blend coleslaw or shredded cabbage

Cooked jasmine rice (for serving)

Crushed peanuts, fresh cilantro, and sliced scallions (for garnish)


Instructions

Step 1: In a bowl, combine sliced cucumbers, rice vinegar, sugar, sesame oil, and sesame seeds. Stir to coat and set aside.

Step 2: In a small bowl, combine soy sauce, sriracha, sweet chili sauce, and honey. Mix well and set aside.

Step 3: In a large bowl, mix ground chicken with hoisin sauce, ginger, and garlic. Let rest for 15 minutes.

Step 4: Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat with a splash of oil. Add marinated chicken and cook until browned, stirring frequently.

Step 5: Add coleslaw or cabbage to the skillet and stir to combine.

Step 6: Pour in the sauce and stir well. Simmer for 3–4 minutes until cabbage wilts slightly.

Step 7: Start with a base of jasmine rice, then top with the chicken mixture.

Step 8: Add cucumber salad, then garnish with crushed peanuts, cilantro, and scallions.

Serve fresh and enjoy!

Spicy Salmon Rice Bowl


Main Ingredients

8 oz salmon fillet, cut into cubes

1 tbsp gochujang paste (for marinade)

1 tbsp maple syrup (for marinade)

1 tbsp avocado oil (for marinade)

2 tbsp white sesame seeds

2 tbsp black sesame seeds

2 tbsp avocado oil (for frying)

1 tbsp gochujang paste (for sauce)

1 tbsp maple syrup (for sauce)

1 tbsp spicy Kewpie mayo or regular mayo

1 tbsp rice vinegar

Cooked jasmine or sushi rice

Roasted vegetables (broccoli, bell peppers, or carrots)

Sesame seeds and sliced scallions (for garnish)


Instructions

Step 1: Cut salmon into bite-sized cubes.

Step 2: In a small bowl, combine 1 tbsp gochujang paste, 1 tbsp maple syrup, and 1 tbsp avocado oil to make the marinade.

Step 3: On a plate, mix the white and black sesame seeds together.

Step 4: Dip each salmon cube into the gochujang mixture, then press into the sesame seeds to form a crust.

Step 5: Heat 2 tbsp avocado oil in a skillet over medium heat. Cook salmon cubes in a single layer for about 4 minutes per side until golden and crispy.

Step 6: While salmon cooks, whisk together gochujang, maple syrup, Kewpie mayo, and rice vinegar to make the drizzle sauce.

Step 7: Roast vegetables with desired seasoning.

Step 8: Serve crispy salmon over rice with roasted vegetables. Drizzle with sauce and garnish with sesame seeds and scallions.

Serve fresh, drizzle extra sauce if needed, and enjoy!
