Berry Cheesecake


Main Ingredients

200g graham crackers or digestive biscuits, crushed

100g melted butter

600g cream cheese, softened

150g granulated sugar

3 large eggs, beaten

100ml heavy cream

1 tsp vanilla extract or seeds from 1 vanilla pod

25g lemon juice

250g fresh mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)

Fresh mint leaves

Powdered sugar


Instructions

Step 1: Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C). Grease a 9-inch springform pan and line the bottom with parchment paper.

Step 2: In a bowl, mix crushed graham crackers with melted butter until combined.

Step 3: Press the mixture firmly into the bottom and about 1 inch up the sides of the pan. Chill for 30 minutes.

Step 4: In a large bowl, beat cream cheese and sugar until smooth.

Step 5: Gradually add beaten eggs, mixing well after each addition.

Step 6: Stir in heavy cream, vanilla, and lemon juice until just combined. Do not overmix.

Step 7: Pour the filling over the chilled crust and smooth the top.

Step 8: Bake for 50–60 minutes until edges are set but center is still slightly jiggly.

Step 9: Turn off oven, crack the door open, and let cheesecake cool in the oven for 1 hour.

Step 10: Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

Top with fresh berries and mint leaves, dust with powdered sugar, serve, and enjoy!

Hot Fudge Brownie Sundae


Main Ingredients

½ cup (1 stick) butter, melted

1 cup granulated sugar

2 large eggs

1 tsp vanilla extract

⅓ cup unsweetened cocoa powder

½ cup all-purpose flour

¼ tsp salt

¼ tsp baking powder

Vanilla ice cream

Hot fudge sauce

Whipped cream

Maraschino cherry (optional)


Instructions

Step 1: Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease an 8x8-inch baking pan.

Step 2: In a bowl, mix melted butter and sugar until well combined.

Step 3: Add eggs and vanilla, stirring until smooth.

Step 4: Add cocoa powder, flour, salt, and baking powder. Mix just until combined — do not overmix.

Step 5: Pour batter into prepared pan and spread evenly.

Step 6: Bake for 20–25 minutes until a toothpick comes out with moist crumbs, not wet batter.

Step 7: Let cool slightly, then cut into squares.

Step 8: Place a warm brownie square in a bowl or sundae dish.

Step 9: Top with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Step 10: Drizzle generously with hot fudge sauce.

Garnish with whipped cream and top with a cherry if desired, serve and enjoy!

Caramel Apple Pie


Main Ingredients

300g plain flour, plus extra for dusting

3 tbsp caster sugar

200g cold salted butter, cut into cubes

1 tsp white wine vinegar

1 egg, beaten (for brushing)

1kg Granny Smith apples, peeled and sliced ½ cm thick

1 lemon, zested and juiced

15g fresh ginger, peeled and grated

1 tsp ground cinnamon

1 tsp mixed spice or pumpkin pie spice

2 tbsp cornflour

200g granulated sugar

85g salted butter

120ml heavy cream

Large pinch of salt

Vanilla ice cream (for serving)


Instructions

Step 1: In a large bowl, mix flour and sugar. Add butter cubes and work into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized pieces remaining.

Step 2: Add vinegar, then slowly add 120ml ice-cold water 1 tablespoon at a time, mixing until a shaggy dough forms.

Step 3: Turn out onto a work surface and gently fold the dough until it comes together. Divide in half, shape into discs, wrap in plastic, and chill for at least 2 hours.

Step 4: In a large bowl, toss apple slices with lemon juice and zest. Add spices and cornflour, mix well, and set aside.

Step 5: In a medium saucepan, heat sugar over low heat, swirling occasionally, until melted and deep amber in color.

Step 6: Remove from heat and stir in butter until melted. Carefully stir in cream and salt — mixture will bubble up. Let cool for 10 minutes.

Step 7: Pour caramel over the apples and stir to coat.

Step 8: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).

Step 9: Roll out one disc of dough into a 12-inch circle and line a 9-inch pie dish. Trim excess.

Step 10: Pour the apple filling into the crust.

Step 11: Roll out the second disc of dough. Place whole over the top with slits cut for steam, or cut into strips for a lattice top.

Step 12: Trim and crimp the edges. Brush the top crust with beaten egg.

Step 13: Place pie on a baking sheet and bake for 20 minutes. Reduce to 375°F and bake for 40–50 minutes more until crust is golden and filling is bubbly.

Step 14: If crust edges brown too quickly, cover with foil or a pie shield.

Step 15: Cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before serving.

Serve warm slices with a scoop of vanilla ice cream and enjoy!
